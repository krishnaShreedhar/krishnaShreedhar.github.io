## What Culture Is Not

Walk into the office of any startup that values culture and you'll find the same inventory:
standing desks, exposed brick, a phrase on the wall about moving fast, and a kitchen full of
snacks. These are props. They are not culture.

> Culture is what happens when the manager is not in the room.

## The Residue of Decisions

**Culture is the accumulated residue of decisions made under pressure.**

When a deadline arrives and you have to choose between shipping a known bug and delaying —
what you choose, consistently, over time, is your culture.

> [!TIP]
> Audit your last five tough decisions as a team. The pattern you find is a more honest
> statement of culture than any value you've written down.

## Three Levers That Actually Work

### 1. What You Celebrate

What gets recognised in public signals what the team values.

### 2. What You Tolerate

Leaders define the floor of acceptable behaviour, not the ceiling.

### 3. Who You Hire and Promote

Deliberately hiring people who challenge existing mental models — then promoting those who
demonstrate the values you want to grow — is the highest-leverage tool available.

## A Note Specific to ML Teams

The team that builds a norm of honest uncertainty outperforms the team that builds a norm of
bravado. Psychological safety isn't a soft goal — it's directly upstream of research quality.
[^1]

## A Practical Starting Point

Start with one small ritual: a weekly five-minute slot where someone shares something they
tried that didn't work and what they learned.
